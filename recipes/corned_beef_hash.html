Corned Beef Hash

Traditional Homemade Corned Beef Hash

The British invented the term “corned beef” in the 17th century to describe the size of the salt crystals used to cure the meat, the size of corn kernels. Although history traces the advent of corned beef hash on American soil to the 19th-century Ashkenazi Jews, the classic American corned beef hash is credited to New England residents in the United States who saw the meal as a means to use up leftovers from dinner.

It's a versatile meal that pairs well with eggs, making it a fantastic brunch, breakfast, or dinner option everyone will love!

Ingredients

  • 4 Tbsp Unsalted butter
  • 1/2 Yellow onion (chopped)
  • 1 tsp Kosher salt
  • 1/2 tsp Coarse ground pepper
  • 2 lbs Russet potatoes (peeled and diced)
  • 2 C Cooked corned beef (shredded)
  • 2 Tbsp Water
  • Scrambled or over-easy eggs (optional)

Instructions

  1. Heat butter in a large case iron skillet over medium heat.
  2. Add onions, potatoes, salt, and pepper. Cook for 5-6 minutes or until potatoes are lightly browned.
  3. Add corned beef and mix well.
  4. Add water and cover for 1-2 minutes.
  5. Top with eggs and serve.
